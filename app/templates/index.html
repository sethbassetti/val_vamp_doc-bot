Overview

The Creation of The DocBot.

What is The DocBot?

We designed The DocBot with the sole purpose of helping individuals seek the professional care they may need for any physical or mental problems they may be experiencing.

What can The DocBot do for its users?

The DocBot will give individuals access to input their current problems into a text box, and from there the bot will process an output containing advice regarding what could be the cause of the user's symptoms, what could help lessen the severity of the user's conflict, and whether or not it is necessary to involve professional care.

What Are the Benefits of The DocBot?

The DocBot grants its users with a vast amount of benefits.For example, The DocBot provides an output containing information regarding what the possibilities of the input could be along with when it is necessary to involve professional medical care. Another Example would be how, in the case of a common cold, The DocBot's advice will not only be helpful for the user, but it will help avoid users from a vast payment to the medical center when faced with a conflict that is not severe.

Dataset

with the help of doctor-patient responses...

Our data set consists of over 250 thousand logged patient-doctor responses. Included are questions ranging from a broken bone to a small headache. Such a large amount of data helps our AI produce a more accurate response! We used Kaggle.com to find our dataset, which was a json file which we converted to csv after some file work. We did this by making headers "prompt" and "completion" and adding the prompts and completions under it. Finally we fed it into the finetuning commands in openai to create a usable file for the bot to train on.

Visit Kaggle!

Training Our Model

teaching our GPT-2 Model

Using our pre-processed dataset and feeding the information to our AI model we waited for it to generate its own text from a user's question. Although it may be a simpler model, the GPT-2 "124M" model processed our data in a short time frame allowing us to begin working on our website. To further improve our model, we choose to fine tune a GPT-3 AI model. This model includes more parameters for the AI to learn from as well as a larger variety of stored topics to help our DocBot.

Making The Website

a balance between HTML, CSS, Python, and JavaScript

We created our website with HTML and included specific styling with CSS. This would be the front-end of the website. To incorporate our trained AI model into a user friendly The DocBot we used Flask and a series of its libraries. With a bit of JavaScript as well, The DocBot was fully ready for use.
